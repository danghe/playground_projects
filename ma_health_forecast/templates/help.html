Market Health (Macro)

The Core Engine
Functional Overview

The Market Health module answers the question: "Is the macro environment conducive to M&A?" It aggregates 5 key economic signals to produce a composite "M&A Temperature" and forecasts deal volume 12 months out.

1. Top-Down Methodology (VAR Model)

We utilize a Vector Autoregression (VAR) model, a stochastic process model used to capture the linear interdependencies among multiple time series. 2. Impulse Response Function (IRF)

The "Shock Simulator" allows users to perturb one variable (e.g., +1 SD shock to VIX) and observe the orthogonalized response of M&A Volume over 12 periods. This quantifies resilience vs. fragility in the current market regime.

Strategic Radar

Sector Monitoring
Bottom-Up Surveillance

While Market Health looks at the forest, Strategic Radar looks at the trees. It scans the entire coverage universe to calculate a Seller Pressure Index (SPI) for every company, identifying those most likely to transact regardless of the macro environment.

1. The SPI Scoring Model (0-100)

The SPI is a composite score derived from 3 weighted operational signals. High scores (>70) indicate a high probability of openness to a sale or activist intervention.

A. Price Dislocation (40%)

Compares current price to 52-week High. Steep drops (>30%) imply shareholder dissatisfaction and vulnerability.

Source: yfinance (Live)
B. Operational Stress (30%)

Measures Leverage (Net Debt/EBITDA) and Growth trajectory. High leverage + Low growth = Forced Seller.

Source: 10-K/10-Q (Fundamental)
C. Strategic Flux (30%)

Analysis of news sentiment, leadership changes, or failed product launches that trigger strategic reviews.

Source: Gemini AI (News Analysis)
2. AI "Quick Takes"

The system uses Gemini 2.0 Flash to read the latest headlines and earnings transcripts for high-SPI targets, summarizing the "Deal Thesis" into a single sentence (e.g., "Shareholder activism driving divestiture of non-core EU assets.").

Deal Command

Execution
Origination & Matchmaking

Deal Command is the "Matchmaker". It moves beyond list-building to Pairwise Analysis, evaluating the specific fit between an Acquirer (You) and the Universe (Targets).

The "Gate & Score" Logic

To avoid wasting time on unrealistic deals, the engine applies a strict funnel before scoring fit.

Stage Test Logic / Threshold
1. Hard Gates Knockout Criteria
  • Materiality: Target Rev > 3% of Acquirer Rev.
  • Affordability: User Total Capacity > Deal Value (or LBO Feasible).
  • Solvency: Combined EBITDA > 0 (No distress-on-distress).
2. Scoring Weighted Fit Score
  • Strategic (40%): Industry overlap, Growth injection, Value Arb.
  • Feasibility (30%): Clean balance sheet, low pro-forma leverage.
  • Close Prob (30%): Willingness (SPI) + Regulatory Risk.

Deal Architect

Strategic Simulation
The "Virtual Sandbox" (Deep Dive)

Deal Architect is the final step. It allows users to "Simulate" a specific deal structure to check viability before going to committee. It uses live rate data to model debt costs.

Model Mechanics: The "Deal Physics"
Metric Calculation Formula Thresholds
Offer EV (Target Market Cap + Net Debt) * (1 + Premium%) Default Premium: 25%
Firepower Coverage (Acquirer Cash + Debt Cap + Stock Cap) / Offer EV > 1.5x = Strong
Pro-Forma Leverage (Acq Debt + Tgt Debt + New Deal Debt) / Combined EBITDA < 4.0x = Healthy
> 4.5x = Breach (Requires Equity Cure)
Close Probability 0.4*Financing + 0.25*Regulatory + 0.35*Willingness < 50% = High Execution Risk

Macro Input: The cost of new debt is anchored dynamically to the 10-Year Treasury Yield (^TNX) plus a credit spread.

2. The AI Intelligence Layer

The platform doesn't just calculate; it reasons. We employ Google Gemini 2.0 Pro to act as a "Virtual Associate", ensuring qualitative risks aren't missed.

"Quick Takes" (Batch)

Instantly scans the top 10 matches to generate a "One-Line Strategic Hook" and a specific Red Flag Risk for each. This helps users skim rapid-fire opportunities without opening every file.

IC Memo Generation

The "Deep Dive" generates a structured Investment Committee memo. It synthesizes the numeric "Deal Physics" with qualitative fit, drafting sections for Strategic Rationale, Synergy Potential, and Execution Risks.
